How To Become A Plastic Shoonya School

To earn the designation of a Plastic Shoonya School, the entire school community—both staff and students—must collaborate on a concise series of straightforward steps aimed at reducing single-use plastic consumption. The primary goal is to eliminate or significantly decrease the use of the Big 4 plastic polluters and other problematic plastics commonly found in school environments.

Target items for reduction or elimination include

Cups and lids

Straws

Bags

Cutlery and crockery

Any other plastic items identified as problematic within the school

Upon achieving Plastic Shoonya Status, schools receive a package of recognition items including:

1. An autographed book

2.A commemorative poster

3. An official certificate

These rewards highlight and celebrate the school's successful efforts to reduce plastic usage.

THE STEPS TO BECOMMING A PLASTIC SHOONYA SCHOOL

Level 1 – INSPIRE

Key Actions and Required Evidence:

  • 1. Awareness Campaign
    • Launch an initiative to inform the school community about your Plastic shoonya School mission
  • 2. Environmental Cleanup
    • Organize and conduct a school-wide litter collection event
  • 3. Video Declaration
    • Create and share a brief video explaining your enthusiasm for the Plastic shoonya School journey

ACTIVATE

Learn about the dangers of plastic pollution and empower your school to take meaningful action!

Level 2: ANALYSE

Key Actions and Required Evidence:

  • 1. School Audit
    • Conduct a comprehensive Plastic Clever Schools assessment
  • 2. Strategic Planning
    • Develop a customized action plan tailored to your school's specific needs and goals
  • This level emphasizes data gathering and strategic preparation to guide your school's plastic reduction initiatives effectively.

ANALYSE

Find the problem plastics in your school and gear up to take steps towards reducing them.

Level 3: ACTION

Key Actions and Required Evidence:

  • 1. Targeted Campaign
    • Implement and execute a campaign addressing at least two problematic plastic items in your school
  • 2. Visual Documentation
    • Provide photographic evidence of your campaign's progress and impact
  • 3. Future-Focused Celebration
    • Produce a video that: a) Celebrates your current achievements b) Outlines your strategy for tackling additional plastic types in upcoming initiatives
  • This level focuses on putting plans into action, documenting tangible results, and setting the stage for continued progress in plastic reduction efforts.

ACTION

Act to eliminate or swap single-use plastics for eco-friendly alternatives.
